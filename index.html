Dr. Ankit Gangwal
Introduction to Layer-two Blockchains
IIIT Hyderabad

11:00am, February 19, 2022

Blockchain is a distributed, decentralized, and generally public digital ledger that records transactions in a tamper-evident fashion. Even after many years since its inception, Blockchain-based systems suffer from low transaction throughput and high latency. To tackle the issue of scalability, researchers from both academia and industry have proposed different solutions to improve underlying working principles of Blockchains, e.g., by improving consensus mechanisms, modifying blocksize, and sharding. However, these modifications either lack backward compatibility or lead to forking. Layer-two solutions are built on top of the parent blockchain. These solutions typically preserve the original trust assumptions of the parent blockchain, reduce transaction load on the underlying blockchain, and are fully backward compatible. This talk focuses on the key layer-two solutions and discusses their key aspects.
